# Working with the General Counsel's Office — FAQ

A quick reference put together by the General Counsel's office for deal teams and portfolio-operations colleagues. If your question is not here, email the GC inbox rather than DMing an individual lawyer — the inbox is monitored and will route you to the right person faster.

**Q: When should I loop in a lawyer on a deal?**

As soon as you have a live situation. There is no such thing as looping in Legal "too early" in our shop, and there are many examples of looping in too late. A two-minute conversation at the term-sheet stage can save a week of rework at definitive documents.

**Q: What is the difference between GC review and Compliance review?**

Short version: the General Counsel's office handles contract, litigation, and regulatory questions. Compliance handles personal-trading pre-clearance, information barriers, anti-money-laundering checks, and political-contribution pre-clearance. There is overlap on regulatory inquiries; when in doubt, flag both and we will sort it out between us.

**Q: Can I use an outside counsel I have a relationship with?**

Only firms on the approved outside-counsel panel may be engaged without written exception. A request to engage a non-panel firm goes through the GC office and usually requires the partner in charge of the matter to sign off.

**Q: What do I do if I receive a subpoena or regulatory inquiry?**

Do not respond. Do not forward it to the team. Walk it, in person or by encrypted email, to the General Counsel or, in her absence, the Deputy General Counsel. Preservation obligations may attach as soon as the inquiry is received.

**Q: How do I get an NDA out the door quickly?**

Our standard NDA is pre-approved and can be sent directly from the deal team with a copy to the GC inbox. Any counterparty-initiated NDA or any modification to our standard requires GC review before signature. Realistic turnaround on a clean counterparty NDA is one business day.

**Q: Who handles portfolio-company employment-law questions?**

Those route to the Portfolio Operations team's dedicated employment counsel, not the firm GC. The portal has the intake form.

**Q: Anything I should know about retention and document management?**

Yes, and it applies to everyone. Work product should live in firm-sanctioned systems with the appropriate retention settings. Email, collaboration chat, and meeting recordings are all captured under firm retention policy. When in doubt, do not delete.
