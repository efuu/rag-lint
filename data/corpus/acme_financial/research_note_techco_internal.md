# Research Note — Project Bluebird (Portfolio Company Internal)

For Investment Committee circulation only. Project Bluebird is the internal code name for a portfolio company in the workflow-automation software vertical; the external name is withheld per information-barrier practice.

## Situation

Bluebird is entering its third year inside the portfolio and is in the middle of a CEO transition announced to the board on December 11. The outgoing CEO has agreed to a twelve-month consulting role; the incoming CEO joined from a larger peer and brings a strong enterprise-sales track record but less product depth than the outgoing founder.

## Operating performance

Trailing-twelve-month ARR of $186 million, up 22 percent year over year. Net revenue retention of 108 percent, down from a peak of 119 percent in 2023. Gross margin held at 76 percent. The ARR growth remains healthy in absolute terms but the deceleration in net retention is the single most important operational metric to watch in 2026.

We attribute the net retention decline to two factors: a slower-than-planned upsell motion in the midmarket segment, and a narrowly scoped but real wave of downsells from a cohort of early customers whose seat counts are settling at a lower steady state than their launch configurations.

## Product and competitive position

Bluebird's core automation platform remains feature-competitive in our diligence view. The sharper question is whether the generative-AI features that the company announced at their July user conference mature quickly enough to reset net retention before the downsell cohort becomes a meaningful percentage of revenue. Management's current plan assumes the first reset by the second half of 2026.

## Financials and capital structure

The company draws on a $65 million revolver at a small portfolio lender; net leverage is 2.1x trailing. Free cash flow has turned modestly positive for the first time in 2025 and management expects to sustain positive FCF through 2026. We do not see a near-term need for additional primary capital.

## Exit considerations

We remain in year three of a target five-year hold. Strategic interest has emerged twice in the last nine months from peers and from one large software platform; we have politely declined to engage in both cases. Our working view is that a 2027 sale process, predicated on the generative-AI reset playing out as management expects, will produce a materially better outcome than taking a strategic conversation today.

## Monitoring

Portfolio operations is scheduled to attend board meetings monthly through the CEO transition period and to reduce to quarterly cadence once the new CEO has completed an operating review. We will circulate a committee note in March after the first full quarter under the new CEO.
