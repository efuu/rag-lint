# Strategy — Excerpt from the 2024 Annual Report

## Letter from the Managing Partner

2024 was a year of steady execution against the multi-year plan we laid out at our 2023 investor day. We grew fee-paying assets under management, closed our fourth flagship credit vehicle on its hard cap, and continued to deepen our sector specialist benches across the firm. We are proud of what the team delivered in a year that asked more of every seat than we had originally budgeted for.

We want to use this letter to do three things: restate the long-term thesis in plain language for our limited partners, describe where we invested behind that thesis during the year, and be honest about the places where the pace of execution has been slower than we would like.

## Our strategic thesis: a disciplined expansion into private credit

Our disciplined expansion into private credit, first announced at the 2023 investor day, has continued to be the central multi-year thesis guiding our capital allocation through 2024. We see the opportunity as structural rather than cyclical, driven by the continued retreat of regulated bank lenders from the middle market and by durable demand from sponsors for flexible, relationship-driven financing.

The build has emphasized sector breadth over speed. We have added senior originators in software, healthcare services, and specialty industrials, and we have deepened our workout and restructuring bench so that we are positioned for a full credit cycle rather than for the benign environment of the past eighteen months.

We have been deliberate about pace. Firms that over-hired into the private-credit theme in 2022 and 2023 are now rationalizing; we have instead grown the origination team by roughly twenty percent year over year, a rate we believe we can sustain through the cycle without compromising underwriting standards. Over the medium term, we continue to evaluate both organic and inorganic paths to scale this franchise.

## Capital formation

Fundraising for our fourth flagship credit vehicle closed in the third quarter. The fund was oversubscribed, and we accepted commitments at the hard cap to preserve deployment discipline. Our limited partner base continues to be heavily weighted toward long-duration institutional capital, with public pensions and sovereign wealth representing the majority of the close.

We also launched an evergreen sleeve targeted at qualified high-net-worth channels. Early reception has been encouraging but we have set a deliberate ramp so that deployment velocity does not outrun our origination bench.

## Portfolio performance

Our seasoned funds continued to perform in line with or ahead of underwriting. Realizations were lower than 2023 as sponsors held assets longer in a repriced exit environment, but credit quality across the book remained stable. The 2021 and 2022 vintages continue to mark to their respective benchmarks.

## Risk management

We have invested in our second-line risk function in proportion to the growth of the origination team, adding portfolio-monitoring analysts and upgrading our data infrastructure so that we can aggregate exposure views by sector, sponsor, and covenant package in something closer to real time.

## Outlook

We enter 2025 with meaningful dry powder, a well-tenured team, and a pipeline that reflects the discipline of the underwriting standard we set at the fund's inception. We are watching the path of short rates and the health of middle-market sponsor balance sheets closely; both will matter more than headlines for the realized returns our limited partners ultimately see.
