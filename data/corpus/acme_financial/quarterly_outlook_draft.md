# Quarterly Outlook — Draft

This draft has not yet been reviewed by Compliance or by the Managing Partner's office. It is shared with the senior deal-team leads for comment ahead of the next investor-relations cycle. Please mark it up in-line rather than rewriting whole sections.

## Macro setup

We enter the second quarter with the path of short rates broadly in line with what we assumed in our last outlook. The dispersion in the term structure has tightened since December, and we are seeing more two-way flow in credit spreads than we have in the prior two quarters. Neither change alters our working base case for the year.

## Deal-flow read

Sponsor activity picked up in late March after a quieter February. We expect that pace to continue into the second quarter, driven largely by sponsors electing to transact rather than continue to carry assets across an uncertain exit window. The mix of deal sizes has skewed modestly larger than the middle of last year; we do not read this as a secular shift.

## Fundraising

Closes in the quarter were in line with the pacing shared at the November limited-partner meeting. We see stability in demand from our repeat limited-partner base and a slight pickup in first-time commitments from insurance channels.

## Portfolio

Across our flagship funds, mark-to-mark performance held in line with underwriting. Defaults across the credit book remained contained. One portfolio-company CEO transition, which we had flagged in the January update, is now underway and progressing on the timeline we had set.

## Outlook caveats

This outlook is provisional. It will be refined after the April review cycle and any material changes will be flagged in a follow-up note. Please do not circulate outside the distribution list at the top of this document.
