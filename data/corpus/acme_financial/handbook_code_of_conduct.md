# Code of Conduct

Acme Capital has built a franchise on the trust that limited partners, portfolio companies, and counterparties place in the firm. This code of conduct describes the standards we expect every employee of the firm to meet in their day-to-day work. It is not a list of rules for rules' sake; it is a working statement of how we want to behave as an organization.

## Integrity in dealings

Every employee is expected to deal honestly and fairly with limited partners, portfolio companies, counterparties, and colleagues. Misrepresenting fund performance, concealing material facts in negotiations, or inflating achievements in an internal review are all incompatible with continued employment at the firm.

## Conflicts of interest

Employees must disclose personal relationships, outside business activities, and personal trading interests that could reasonably create a conflict with the firm's fiduciary obligations. Disclosure is made through the annual compliance attestation and on a rolling basis whenever new circumstances arise. The Compliance team's judgment governs whether a disclosed situation requires a control, recusal, or other mitigation.

## Gifts and entertainment

Gifts and entertainment above the threshold published in the Compliance handbook require pre-clearance. The bar is not whether an item is lavish in the abstract; the bar is whether a reasonable outside observer would view it as capable of influencing a business decision.

## Inside information

Employees who come into possession of material non-public information about a public company, whether through portfolio-company work, industry conversations, or otherwise, must treat that information as restricted and route any questions to Compliance before acting, trading, or sharing further. The firm's information-barrier procedures are described in the Compliance handbook.

## Respectful workplace

The firm will not tolerate harassment, discrimination, or retaliation. Concerns should be raised to a manager, to Human Resources, to the General Counsel, or through the firm's anonymous ethics hotline, any of which will be treated as a legitimate channel.

## Reporting concerns

An employee who in good faith raises a concern about a possible violation of this code is protected against retaliation regardless of whether the concern is ultimately substantiated. The firm expects managers to treat concerns raised to them as a priority item, not as an interruption to their normal workload.

## Acknowledgment

Each employee is asked to read this code at the start of employment and to acknowledge it annually through the compliance attestation portal. Acknowledgment is not the important part; reading and internalizing it is.
