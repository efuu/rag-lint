# Vendor Contract Summary — Q4 2025 Renewals

Prepared by the Vendor Management Office for internal use by the Chief Operating Officer and the General Counsel.

## Overview

Nine enterprise-tier vendor contracts are up for renewal in the first quarter of 2026. This summary captures the commercial terms currently in negotiation and flags the contracts where material changes to scope, price, or risk allocation are expected versus the incumbent agreement.

## Market-data and research

Our primary market-data provider has proposed a three-year extension at a 6 percent annual increase, slightly ahead of published list-price inflation. We are counter-proposing a flat year-one with escalators tied to user-seat growth above a baseline threshold. Our fallback is a two-year renewal at 4 percent annual, which we believe is achievable.

A secondary research platform has proposed consolidating three separately priced modules into a single site license at a headline discount of 11 percent. The arithmetic depends on our projected user count for 2026; procurement is still collecting the inputs from the coverage teams.

## Collaboration and productivity

Our document-collaboration and messaging platform is at renewal. The incumbent is proposing a cross-product discount conditioned on adoption of their identity-provider module. We have declined on security-architecture grounds and expect a conventional single-product renewal at a modest single-digit increase.

## Investment operations software

The back-office platform supporting fund accounting and NAV production is entering the final year of its current contract, with an evergreen renewal trigger if no notice is given by the end of Q1 2026. We are preparing a detailed comparison to two alternative platforms to run in parallel with the incumbent's renewal conversation. Our objective is not to switch, but to improve our negotiating position on the renewal.

## Governance

Material deviations from the prior contract terms — price increases above the inflation baseline, any reduction in service levels, or any new indemnity position — are flagged to the General Counsel for review before countersignature. The VMO will circulate a revised summary after the January procurement committee.
