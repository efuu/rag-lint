# Acceptable Use Policy — Unredacted Draft (Pre-Legal Review)

This policy describes how employees of the firm are expected to use firm-provided technology and networks, and what activities are not permitted on them. It applies to all full-time employees, interns, and long-term contractors issued firm equipment or credentials.

## Scope

This policy applies to firm-issued laptops, phones, and tablets; to firm email, messaging, and document-collaboration accounts; and to any personal device used to access firm systems under the firm's bring-your-own-device program. It applies on firm premises, in client offices, and when working remotely.

## Acceptable use

Firm-issued equipment and credentials are provided for the purpose of carrying out firm business. Limited personal use is permitted where it does not interfere with work, does not consume material firm resources, and does not violate any other provision of this policy or of the code of conduct.

Employees should store firm work product only in firm-sanctioned systems. Use of unsanctioned cloud-storage or file-transfer services for firm work product is not permitted, including for convenience during travel.

## Prohibited activities

The following activities are expressly prohibited on firm equipment and firm networks: installation of software not on the firm's approved list; access to pornographic or gambling content; use of peer-to-peer file-sharing tools; circumvention of firm security controls, including personal VPN services not issued by the firm; and any use that violates applicable law.

## Password and credential hygiene

All firm accounts must use passwords generated by the firm's password manager and must be protected by the firm's multi-factor authentication. Sharing of credentials between employees is not permitted under any circumstance, including within the same team.

## Email and messaging

Firm email is subject to retention and to review under the firm's records-management policy. Employees should assume that firm email may be read by Compliance in the course of periodic surveillance. Personal email accounts should not be used to send, receive, or forward firm business communications.

## Incident reporting

Suspected compromise of a firm account, lost or stolen firm equipment, and suspected phishing messages should be reported to the IT helpdesk as promptly as circumstances allow. The helpdesk can be reached through the internal portal or through the support address published on the intranet.
