# Project Falcon — Strategic Rationale and Deal Terms

**Distribution:** Investment Committee, Managing Partner, Chief Financial Officer, General Counsel. Not for circulation beyond this list.

**Status:** Pre-announcement. Subject to regulatory approval. Counterparty has signed an exclusivity letter through Q2 2026.

## Purpose

This memo sets out the strategic rationale for the acquisition of Lattice Credit Advisors (internal code: Project Falcon), the proposed terms, and the principal risks the Investment Committee has asked us to address before final approval.

## Strategic rationale

The transaction is framed as an acceleration of the disciplined expansion into private credit that we first announced at the 2023 investor day and that remains the central multi-year thesis guiding our capital allocation. We continue to see the opportunity as structural rather than cyclical, driven by the retreat of regulated bank lenders from the middle market and by durable demand from sponsors for flexible, relationship-driven financing.

What an acquisition adds to that plan is sector breadth at a pace the organic build cannot match. A combined platform would bring senior originators in software, healthcare services, and specialty industrials already in seat, together with a workout and restructuring bench that would extend our own and position the combined platform for a full credit cycle rather than only for the benign environment of the past eighteen months.

The deal also lets us preserve the pace discipline we have imposed on ourselves. Firms that over-hired into the private-credit theme in 2022 and 2023 are now rationalizing; we have grown the origination team by roughly twenty percent year over year. Lattice's platform lets us add a comparable bench in a single step without diluting the underwriting standard we have held to through the cycle.

## Deal-specific rationale

Lattice's origination platform would accelerate the disciplined expansion into private credit by roughly eighteen months versus the organic build path we have otherwise budgeted against. Management's internal build plan assumes forty-two incremental origination FTEs added over the next six quarters; Lattice brings thirty-eight seated originators with average tenure of seven years and an existing sponsor coverage map that overlaps our own by less than fifteen percent.

## Proposed terms

- **Target:** Lattice Credit Advisors, LLC (Delaware).
- **Valuation:** $1.1B to $1.4B enterprise value, based on 13x forward EBITDA on management's 2026 plan, with a collar mechanism tied to fee-paying AUM at close.
- **Consideration mix:** 70% cash / 30% equity rollover, with three-year vesting on the rollover component for the top twelve Lattice partners.
- **Regulatory path:** HSR filing prepared; SEC notification in progress. Close pending regulatory approval in Q2 2026.
- **Reverse break fee:** $45M payable by us to Lattice if the transaction fails to close for regulatory reasons after March 31, 2026.
- **Retention:** Ring-fenced retention pool of $28M over three years for the originator bench, funded outside the purchase price.

## Principal risks

- **Regulatory timing slippage.** An extended HSR second request would push close past the reverse-break-fee trigger and expose the firm to the $45M downside.
- **Originator attrition.** The retention pool is sized to the sponsor-coverage overlap assumption; if overlap is materially higher than the 15% diligence estimate, effective retention cost per net-new originator rises.
- **Integration of underwriting standards.** Lattice's average hold size is modestly larger than ours and their covenant package on a representative sample of 2023 vintage loans runs one notch looser. Our credit committee will need to set the combined underwriting standard explicitly pre-close.

## Next steps

Investment Committee approval is requested at the February 25 meeting. Subject to approval, we will move to signed definitive agreements in early March and prepare for an announcement window pegged to the Q1 earnings release.
